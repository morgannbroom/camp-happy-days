Research

I conducted research on Camp Happy Days that included primary resarch on the client themselves, secondary reaserch, as well as interviews with key players.

Primary client research

Camp Happy Days is an organization focused on providing cost free programs and events for children and families who are battling or have battled cancer “Our goal is to improve the physical, emotional and psychological health of the entire family facing pediatric cancer.”

WEBSITE

Website/Logo Colors:

  • Yellow, Red, Blue (Logo)
  • Teal, White, Blue, Red (Website/Type)

Common Themes/Promotion:

  • Donate
  • Volunteer

Common Design Elements:

  • Photography/Videography of camp
  • Centered blocks of text/Centered Headings

Inconsistencies Within the Design:

  • Crossover elements between pages (How to volunteer or donate on pages not related to volunteering or donating)
  • Different from their logo/brand colors
  • A lot of copy to read through
  • Sometimes is necessary, but for some users this could result in them not wanting to read that much and clicking off website
  • Repetitive in message
  • Needs to be condensed/shortened/not used on every page of the website

SOCIAL MEDIA

Instagram:

  • Gold Theme (Profile Picture and Highlights)
  • For a Pediatric Cancer Awareness, but not mentioned/used on website
  • Promotes Gala
  • Pediatric Cancer Awareness
  • Camp/Kids
  • Donations

Facebook:

  • Gold Theme (Profile Picture and Header)
  • Same as Instagram
  • Different handle than instagram
  • Inconsistent
  • Posts a lot of photos and videos
  • Promotions similar/same as Instagram

Vimeo:

    Posts camp videos

*Twitter icon is used on website, but leads to nothing. This social media platform seems to not be in use anymore or is not their primary/preferred way of communicating. The Icon needs to be removed.

Problems with Social Media:

  • No apparent theme to posting
  • Color of Feed
  • Types of Photos
  • Quality of Photos are poor
  • Not a lot of photos of the camp or of children (Instagram)
  • Promotions are posted as flyers designed in all different types of ways
  • Nothing is branded
  • No consistent color theme

Secondary Research

News/Benefits of Camp for Kids with Cancer

News

  • Specific and personalized stories from campers
  • About specific foundations goals to help kids with cancer through camp
  • Events/galas put on by camps

Benefits of Camp

  • Reduce anxiety
  • Physical changes to the body
  • Increased self-perception
  • Families feel validated, affirmed, and less alone

“Competitors”

Direct

  • Limited copy
  • Designated pages to get involved and/or donate
  • Clear message on homepage

Indirect

  • Minimalistic
  • Clean logo/type
  • Muted color palettes

Interviews

Client

For my client interview I spoke with Emily Juszczyk who is the Camp & Programs Manager of Camp Happy Days. She is also directly over the social media and website of the organization. She was very helpful and talked to me for as long as I needed.

From the Interview with Emj, I learned that she also felt like there was a lot of copy on their site. She felt that things could be better organized or condensed, just as I had been feeling. This was fantastic hearing her say it without me having to bring it up at all. She also felt like a lot of the programs they offer aren’t being highlighted enough or are overshadowed. These comments and thoughts will help me figure out the best way to condense the information and highlight what’s truly important.

User

I interviewed Ryan Rogers, a 20 year old childhood friend of a kid who went to Camp Happy Days as my user. He is a potential person that would like to use Camp Happy Day's website to sign up and volunteer.

Ryan knows about this organization by word of mouth, which is what Emj said is the main way people hear about it. He didn’t need a whole explanation about camp and volunteering in order to sign up. He had to scroll a lot and figure out which button was the right one in order to begin the application process. This could cause potential volunteers to click off the site and not sign up at all.
