Design Challenge

First, we started with determining the design challenge. This is a worksheet from the Human Centered Design Toolkit helps frame the challenge as an actionable question.

What is the problem you’re trying to solve?

Provide cost free camps and events to children who have battled and/or are continuing to battle cancer Frame the design question:

How can Camp Happy days get more campers, volunteers, and donations through their website? State the impact you’re trying to have?

I want there to be an increase in sign-ups for the camp, for both campers and volunteers as well as an increase in donations given to the organization.

What are some possible solutions to your problem?

Condensing information within tabs (taking away or combining some tabs), making specific goals evident on the homepage, creating a more user-friendly design that shortens their time trying to find important information, minimizing distracting design elements (unnecessary colors, script type, excess photography).

What are some of the context and constraints that you’re facing?

This is a small camp, based only in Charleston. They don’t do much advertising and have very little branding. May be difficult to access photography and or video material.

Does your original question need a tweak?

How can the Camp Happy Days website easily provide information about the camp, volunteering, and donating to the families/friends of children dealing with cancer?
